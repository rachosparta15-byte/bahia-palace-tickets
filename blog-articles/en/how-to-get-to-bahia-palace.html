Bahia Palace sits in the southern medina, about 1.2 km southeast of Jemaa el-Fna. It sounds close, and it is — but the medina's tangle of unmarked alleys trips up first-timers constantly. The good news is that the route is one of the more straightforward walks in the medina, using streets wide enough to navigate with a map. Morocco's Ministry of Culture manages the palace at Rue Riad Zitoun el Jedid (minculture.gov.ma), and the entrance is well-signed once you're on the right street. Here's everything you need to get there without getting lost.

Key Takeaways

  • The walk from Jemaa el-Fna takes 12 to 18 minutes. The route is two streets: Rue Riad Zitoun el Kedim heading south, then Rue Riad Zitoun el Jedid. Download Google Maps offline before leaving your accommodation.
  • A petit taxi costs 15 to 25 MAD by meter. Drivers will quote 50 to 80 MAD to tourists — negotiate before you get in, agree on a price, then get in. All taxis know "Palais Bahia."
  • The palace entrance is a set of large wooden doors on the left side of the street, marked by a sign in Arabic and French. It's easy to walk past if you're moving quickly. Check your left side in the final 200 metres.

Where Exactly Is Bahia Palace in Marrakech?

Address: Rue Riad Zitoun el Jedid, Marrakech 40000, Morocco.

GPS: 31.6226° N, 7.9827° W

Bahia Palace sits in the Mellah quarter of Marrakech's southern medina. It's not visible from any main road, and the entrance is on a street that runs between two larger medina arteries. The entrance is a set of large wooden doors on the left side of the street — elegant but understated. First-time visitors regularly walk past it because the doors blend into the streetscape.

Save the GPS coordinates or the address to your phone before you leave your accommodation. Having the destination already loaded in Google Maps means you can navigate without searching for a signal in a narrow alley.

How Do You Walk from Jemaa el-Fna to Bahia Palace?

Walking is the best option. The route takes you through the real medina — past craft workshops, spice stalls, and local tea houses — and it's part of the Marrakech experience.

  1. Stand in Jemaa el-Fna with the Koutoubia Mosque on your right (west side). Face east toward the medina entrance.
  2. Enter the medina and take Rue Riad Zitoun el Kedim heading south. This is a relatively wide covered lane — look for the sign at the entrance or set your GPS to walking mode.
  3. Walk south for about 10 minutes. You'll pass souvenir shops, small cafés, and craftsmen. Keep going straight.
  4. The lane merges into Rue Riad Zitoun el Jedid. Stay on this road heading south.
  5. Bahia Palace entrance appears on your left, roughly 200m after the merge. If you reach the Mellah market, you've gone 100m too far — turn back north.

Pro tip: Download Google Maps offline for Marrakech before you leave your hotel. The medina is mapped well enough for walking navigation. Set the app to walking mode and follow the blue line — driving directions are useless inside the medina's pedestrian lanes.

What you'll see on the walk: The route along Rue Riad Zitoun el Kedim passes one of Marrakech's more authentic commercial medina streets — carpet dealers, leather goods, and spice shops that cater to local customers as much as tourists. The street is relatively straight and wide by medina standards, with occasional shade. In our experience, visitors who walk rather than take a taxi arrive at the palace having already absorbed something of the medina atmosphere, which makes the transition inside more natural.

How Much Does a Taxi Cost to Bahia Palace?

Marrakech's petit taxis are small red cars (usually Fiats) that run on meters — in theory. In practice, drivers often quote a fixed price to tourists.

  • Fair meter price: 15–25 MAD from Jemaa el-Fna
  • What drivers will quote first: 50–80 MAD — negotiate firmly, agree before getting in
  • Tell the driver "Palais Bahia" — every driver knows it
  • Taxis can't enter the deepest medina lanes. They'll drop you at the nearest accessible point on Rue Riad Zitoun el Jedid — a 2 to 3 minute walk from the gate

If you're using a ride-hailing app, both Careem and inDrive operate in Marrakech and show fares upfront. More reliable for price transparency than street-hailing. Particularly useful for the return journey when you're tired and less inclined to negotiate.

Caleche (Horse-Drawn Carriage)

Caleches line up near Jemaa el-Fna and around the city walls. They're slow, scenic, and a legitimate way to travel if you're not in a rush.

  • One-way price to Bahia Palace: 60–100 MAD (negotiate before boarding)
  • They drop you at the edge of the pedestrian medina zone — same short walk to the gate
  • Worth it for the experience on a first visit; impractical if you have a packed schedule or are visiting in summer heat

Which Google Maps Settings Work Best in the Medina?

  • Download offline maps for Marrakech before leaving your accommodation (Maps app → search Marrakech → Download). GPS works without a data connection.
  • Set navigation to walking mode — driving directions are useless inside the medina
  • When the app says "you've arrived" and you see nothing, look left — the entrance doors are easy to walk past
  • Zoom in to street level in the final 200m for accuracy
  • What3words is a useful backup: every 3m² has a unique address and works offline. The app uses "///noble.brave.tones" (or similar) as the palace entrance's unique code — save it before you go.

What Other Sites Are Worth Visiting Near Bahia Palace?

Bahia Palace is well-placed for a half-day or full-day medina loop. Morocco's 17.4 million annual tourists (ONMT, Morocco Tourism Annual Report 2024) know this southern medina corner as one of the richest clusters of historic sites in the country.

  • Saadian Tombs — 8-minute walk south. The Saadian dynasty's royal mausoleum, sealed for 200 years. One of the finest examples of Islamic architecture in Morocco. Entry: 100 MAD (foreign adult). See the combined half-day guide.
  • El Badi Palace — 12-minute walk west. Atmospheric 16th-century ruins. Entry: 100 MAD (foreign adult). See the Bahia vs El Badi comparison.
  • The Mellah — directly adjacent. Marrakech's old Jewish quarter, with a covered food market, the Lazama Synagogue, and a different pace from the main souks.
  • Place des Ferblantiers — 3 minutes west. The lantern-makers' square.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is the walk from Jemaa el-Fna to Bahia Palace?

At a normal pace, the walk takes 12 to 18 minutes. It covers about 1.2 km along Rue Riad Zitoun el Kedim and Rue Riad Zitoun el Jedid. Allow a few extra minutes on your first visit to account for wrong turns or pausing to look at the medina along the way.

Is it easy to find Bahia Palace on your own?

Yes, if you use Google Maps or follow the route above. The road to the palace is one of the more straightforward routes in the medina. The main mistake is walking past the entrance: the wooden doors are large but blend into the street. Look for the sign above them and check your left side in the final 200 metres.

Can I drive to Bahia Palace?

You can't drive into the medina. If you have a car, park at Parking El Badi (on Place des Ferblantiers, about 600m west — free or very cheap) or Parking Mellah (just south of the Mellah market, a 5-minute walk from the gate). The parking guardian will collect 5 to 10 MAD when you leave — that's normal, not a scam.

Once I'm there, do I need to queue for a ticket?

If you haven't pre-booked, yes — the single ticket window can have a 30 to 45 minute queue in peak season (March through November). Book in advance to walk straight in. See the entrance fee guide for prices and booking options, and the first-visit tips for everything to prepare before arriving.
