The official ticket price to enter Bahia Palace is 100 MAD, roughly €9 or $10 USD. By international standards, that's a bargain for one of the most beautifully preserved Islamic palaces in the world. But the number on the ticket isn't the real cost. The real cost is the 30 to 45 minutes spent standing in line at the single ticket booth, in the heat, watching your sightseeing window shrink.

Key Takeaways

  • The official entrance fee set by Morocco's Ministry of Culture (minculture.gov.ma) is 100 MAD for foreign adult visitors. Children under 7 enter free.
  • Payment at the door is cash only. No card machines are available at the ticket window. Bring dirhams.
  • Booking online is the single most effective thing you can do to improve your visit. A pre-booked ticket removes the entire queue risk and guarantees your entry window.

What Is the Official Entrance Fee for Bahia Palace in 2026?

As of 2026, the standard entrance fee is set by Morocco's Ministry of Culture (minculture.gov.ma). The dual pricing system, where Moroccan nationals pay a reduced rate, is standard practice across state-managed cultural sites in Morocco.

Payment at the door is cash only. There are no card machines at the ticket window. The nearest ATMs are a short walk away near Rue Riad Zitoun el Kedim. Come prepared with dirhams.

Are There Discounts at Bahia Palace?

Students

There is no universally applied student discount for foreign visitors. Moroccan students with a valid national student card may access a reduced rate, but international student cards such as the ISIC are not reliably honoured at the door. Foreign visitors should expect to pay the full adult price of 100 MAD.

Children

Children under 7 enter free. Foreign children aged 7–13 pay 50 MAD, half the adult rate. Moroccan children pay 10 MAD regardless of age. A family of two foreign adults and two children aged 7–13 pays 300 MAD total, still very affordable for roughly 90 minutes in one of Morocco's finest palaces.

Moroccan Nationals

Morocco operates a dual pricing system at many state-run cultural sites. Bahia Palace is no exception: Moroccan nationals pay 30 MAD versus 100 MAD for foreign visitors. On Fridays and the first day of national or religious holidays, entry is free for Moroccan nationals.

Group Visits

There is no formal group discount at the standard ticket window for foreign visitors. Organised tour groups negotiate rates separately through licensed tour operators. If you're travelling independently, expect to pay 100 MAD per adult regardless of group size.

Is the Skip-the-Line Ticket Worth It?

The honest answer is yes, particularly between March and November. During peak travel season, the queue at Bahia Palace's single ticket window can stretch to 30 to 45 minutes. A skip-the-line ticket lets you bypass that queue entirely. You show your QR code at a dedicated entrance and walk straight in.

When it's worth it: March through May (spring high season), September through October (autumn high season), any public holiday or long weekend, or if you're on a tight schedule with other sites the same day such as the Saadian Tombs, which are ten minutes away.

When it's less critical: Weekday mornings in January or February, or if you arrive right at 9:00 AM before tour groups appear.

The price difference between a door ticket and an online option is modest. The time you save is real. In a city as rewarding as Marrakech, an extra 40 minutes means another landmark, another souk, or a longer lunch. In our experience, visitors who book in advance consistently rate the overall experience higher, because they arrive calm rather than frustrated from a long queue in direct sun.

What's Included in the Ticket?

Whether you buy at the door or online, your ticket covers full access to the palace's main courtyards, reception halls, gardens, and the former harem quarters — self-guided exploration at your own pace across roughly 8,000 m² of 19th-century Moroccan architecture.

Not included: audio guide (available for hire separately at the entrance), guided tour with a licensed local guide (prices vary), or any photography permit. Standard photography is free and allowed throughout the palace without flash.

What Does 100 MAD Buy You?

One useful way to think about the entrance fee: Bahia Palace covers roughly 8,000 m² of open visitor space across multiple courtyards, reception halls, former harem quarters, and gardens. The hand-painted cedar ceilings, hand-cut zellige tilework, and Carrara marble columns were executed by thousands of skilled craftsmen from Fez and represent some of the finest 19th-century Islamic interior work in Morocco. The full history of Ba Ahmed and the palace puts the scale of the achievement in context.

At 100 MAD, the entry is cheaper than Marrakech's Majorelle Garden (150 MAD) and competitive with the Saadian Tombs (70 MAD) while offering considerably more floor space and content. You can dress appropriately — see the dress code guide for what to wear — and plan to spend around 75 to 90 minutes inside.
